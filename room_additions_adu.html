The Addition & ADU Process

A seamless path to expanding your home.

1

Feasibility & Design

We start by assessing your property's potential. Our experts check zoning laws, setback requirements, and lot coverage to determine exactly what can be built. Then, our architects draft custom plans that seamlessly blend with your existing home's style.

2

Permitting & Approval

Navigating Irvine's building department can be complex. We handle the entire submission process, responding to plan checks and securing all necessary permits so you don't have to worry about the paperwork.

  • Architectural Blueprints

3

Foundation & Build

From laying the foundation to framing, roofing, and installing utilities, our crews work efficiently and safely. We keep the job site clean and communicate progress regularly, turning raw materials into your new living space.

Final Inspection

We manage all city inspections to ensure every aspect of code compliance is met. Once approved, we hand over the keys to your brand-new addition or ADU, ready for tenants, family, or your own enjoyment.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to your questions about ADUs and additions.

How much value does an ADU add to my property?
While market conditions vary, an ADU typically adds significant value‚Äîoften 20-30% to your property value. Additionally, it offers the potential for monthly rental income, which can offset construction costs over time.
What are the setback requirements for a backyard ADU in Irvine?
Generally, detached ADUs require a setback of 4 feet from the side and rear property lines. However, rules can differ based on your specific zoning, lot size, and presence of easements. We perform a detailed feasibility study to confirm exact requirements for your lot.
How long does it take to build a room addition?
Room additions typically take 3 to 6 months to complete, depending on size and complexity. This includes framing, roofing, electrical, plumbing, and finishing. The permitting phase prior to construction can also take several weeks to a few months depending on the city's backlog.
